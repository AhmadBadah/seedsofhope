A hard decision to take

When the war started, we were asked, like all the ghazans in the North, to evacuate. But honestly, I didn't know where to go, or how. I can't say that we are used to the attacks, because you never get used to them, but I never thought they would last so long and be so hard. It was difficult to decide to stay, but it would have been also difficult to decide to evacuate. We are not displaced, not literally: we are still in our house and in our neighborhood, but recognizing the streets and the beauty that is hidden under all these layers of dust and rubble is sometimes impossible. Sometimes, when the bombing or artillery is particularly close, the children and I move to the empty houses of our relatives, those who have gone South, in areas that we believe to be temporarily safer but the truth is that it is all a coincidence, being alive or dead today in Gaza is not something that can be calculated or predicted.

Arwa Badah

Arabic Teacher, mother of three

Meet My Kids

Majed

9 years old

His patience in these dark times is a gift

Majed

Majed is the eldest brother among his siblings. He is 9 years old. He has always loved to go to school, I still remember his excitement in the first days, couldn't wait to learn how to read! He still loves reading a lot, and always gets the best marks, but it is not about competition, it is about loving school. He misses his schoolmates and his friends, they used to spend many afternoon together, here, doing PlayStation games. Majed is also a swimmer! I used to take all the kids to the swimming pool and to the sea, one of our favorite place here. He is a kind kid, always ready to spread his love, but there is something that he hates and fears: the sound of planes, the sudden blasts, the bombings. But he is also brave and he never stopped to dream of safety.

Osama

8 years old

His lively mood makes us believe in a brighter future

Osama

Osama, the middle brother, is 8 years old. Just as his brother, he loves school and his favorite subject is Arabic. I am so proud of it, because I am an Arabic teacher, and his questions, his way to go through grammar and vocabulary are amazing to see. His like to go swimming with his brothers but he also loves cycling. Since we did not flee, he has his bicycle, and he always asks permission to ride it. Sometimes, when the afternoon seems quiet, I let him take a ride under my watch, and I see his childhood emerging through his smile. He is also an artist, since he likes drawing, and I love the way he mixes with people: he has a way, a social brighteness that I myself admire a lot. None with a kind heart like his can be at ease with the loud sounds and noise we are undergoing.

Abdul Rahman

4 years old

My little boy reminding us that childhood is strong in their hearts

Abdul Rahman

Abdul Rahman is only three years old, and we all call him Abood. Since he is the little one, he is cuddled by his brothers. He loves to spend the time with them and sharing their interests, so you can see him drawing with Osama, or trying to use the Play Station with Majed. Together they are adorable. He is so young, and he is going through this difficult times that are so painful even for grown-up. At times he asks me why can't we go to the amusement park or to the sea, or why none is selling candies in the markets. Abood is smart, he doesn't need any answer to these questions, he is only three but he knows what war is.

WHY WE NEED HELP

Living in Gaza has never been easy, but before October we had managed to find our happiness. We had a normal life, which sounds so special nowadays. We could work, cultivate our friendships, have a walk on the beach, hang out with our family, and give to our kids and nephews all they needed, and a bit more. We had dreams and plans, and we never thought that our dream would turn out to be a survival one. We don’t know how long we can survive in this catastrophe, and if we are not killed by shelling, starvation will. As you may know, we depend on aids, but sadly the aids are not entering the Strip. Supplies (any kind of supplies) are not enough, and this raises the price of everything. Food, medicines, water, but also baby diapers, and even summer clothing for our children: without your help, we could not afford those expenses. And then, there is the future: what will we do when this will be over? We want to walk on our legs, but I understand that we have to ask and accept your help to get back on the path of our lives. Everything you could do for us will contribute to our future. Thank you
